Samsung Galaxy Tab 2 custom ROMs and thoughts

Posted by Reece Petersen on Saturday, May 22, 2021

Installing roms on Samsung galaxy tab 2 and thoughts on the device

Recently I bought a second hand samsung galaxy tab 2 as a way to get some coding and reading done in more places.

It’s size is only 2.5 inches smaller than my smallest laptop, which is also my main workhorse (Thinkpad x260).

I did not like the original ROM running on it, so I decided to see if more performance could be squeezed out of it.

Installed Omnirom on it because, the omnilauncher as well as its shortcuts and tools felt amazing on my Redmi Note 2.

That did not work well. Afar a while, might have been to Syncthing and my lack of comprehensive ignore rules.

After about a day of being on the screen would not be able to be unlocked. In order to use it, I would have to reboot it.

Then it would stop working soon after again.

Tried with different ROMS, eventually settled on SlimRom and this is the best fit so far, system has been stable for about a year now.

I’m able to read on it, run termux, watch some video, x265 is mostly out of the question due to lack of hardware acceleration support and weak internals.

H264 is workable, but it cannot run at higher than 720p comfortably

Instructions for setting up a custom ROM on the Tab 2

  1. Download odin

  2. Download twrp

  3. connect tab to pc

  4. boot into recovery mode

  5. flash twrp

  6. hold volume up and power while booting to get to twrp

  7. copy roms to base of storage

  8. reboot into twrp

  9. wipe

  10. install
