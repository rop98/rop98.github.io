Backing up and compressing phone pictures

Posted by Reece Petersen on Thursday, April 22, 2021

Backing up and moving all the media on my phone to the external hard drive

When your computer/phone is unresponsive due to the large number of files it might work to use adb, slightly more complicated due to lack of some of the coreutils.

Another solution is to use a server app and connect via the web interface and copy over that way. I like to use X-plore

Normal way would be to plug it in via USB and copy over that way.

Most reliable way would be to store your images on a micro SD card known to not get faulty and work through that.

In my experience 2 of my Micro SD cards have failed me over the course of 3 to 5 years each. 16GB and 32GB respectively.

Steps to reduce space

  1. Copy dcim
  2. Copy opencamera
  3. Copy screenshots
  • in dcim to squash jpgs
mogrify -strip -interlace Plane -sampling-factor 4:2:0 -quality 85% --skip-if-larger  *.jpg
  • in screenshots to squash pngs
mogrify -filter Triangle -define filter:support=2 -unsharp 0.25x0.08+8.3+0.045 -dither None -posterize 136 -quality 82 -define png:compression-filter=5 -define png:compression-level=9 -define png:compression-strategy=1 -define png:exclude-chunk=all -interlace none -colorspace sRGB *.png
  • in screenshots or DCIM to squash video files
# x264, faster but less quality and less of a difference in size
for f in *.mp4;do ffmpeg -i ${f} -vcodec libx264 -crf 28 small264_${f};done;

# x265, slower but less of a quality difference and more of a difference in size
for f in *.mp4;do ffmpeg -i ${f} -vcodec libx265 -crf 28 small_${f};done;
