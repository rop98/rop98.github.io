Obvious ones

Double Jump - Learned from ‘TheBaldChick - Godot 3: How to make your character double jump’

2020-05-22 This took forever. I’ve been trying to add double jump to any game since I downloaded godot.

I learned a lot just going through the basics and now I understand the underlying logic a bit more.

Learning from multiple sources is a good and bad thing.

If I try to follow just one way of doing it, it will be better for that one way but my knowledge is limited in breadth.

If I learn how to implement something in a variety of ways, I actually gain the knowledge and experience to figure things out in varying contexts, but it takes longer.

Like I learned about how it would be a performance gain to only check and only set if on floor and jump count when it is required not every time a player is on the floor.

I learned about how gravity works in godot and how it is different from the cartesian plane in maths.

  • the upper left is considered 0,0
  • going down the y-axis increases the y value

Slide

2020-05-23 A wall slide is also requiring raycasts and the slowing down of gravity. General jump physics requires that the player has gravity applied at the same rate when falling. Slide will have the gravity lessened while raycasts touch the wall.

In order to be accurate, there needs to be a way to determine if it is a high enough wall as slopes and small walls will cause the player to hug to the wall or wall jump instead of regular jump.

Dash and Air Dash

2020-05-23 A dash requires that y gravity be suspended for a timer of 0.5 and then reactivated. During this time of gravity suspension the player will have increased x-axis speed then promptly fall.
